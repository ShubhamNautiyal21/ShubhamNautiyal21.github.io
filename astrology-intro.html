Basics of Vedic Astrology

  • Grahas (Planets): Sun (Self, Soul), Moon (Mind, Emotions), Mars (Energy, Courage), Mercury (Communication, Intellect), Jupiter (Wisdom, Expansion), Venus (Love, Beauty), Saturn (Discipline, Karma), plus Rahu & Ketu (lunar nodes) representing karmic paths and transformation.
  • Rashis (Zodiac Signs): 12 signs—Aries, Taurus, Gemini, Cancer, Leo, Virgo, Libra, Scorpio, Sagittarius, Capricorn, Aquarius, Pisces. Each modifies how planet’s energy expresses itself.
  • Bhavas (Houses): 12 sectors of life: self-identity, wealth, communication, home, creativity, service, partnerships, transformation, travel, career, gains, losses. Planets in houses show where life themes manifest.
  • Nakshatras (Lunar Mansions): The Moon’s path divided into 27 segments—each with mythological, psychological, and timing significance.
  • Dashas & Transits: Dashas are long-term planetary periods that govern major life phases; transits are current movements of planets affecting daily life and triggering events.

John H. Nelson’s Planetary Propagation Theory

John H. Nelson was an engineer in the 1950s who proposed that **planetary alignments**—specifically certain heliocentric angles between planets—correlate with solar activity (sunspots, magnetic storms) which then influence radio wave propagation on Earth. Although the idea is not part of mainstream science, it's intriguing as a pattern-based theory.

  • Key angles: Conjunction (0°), Opposition (180°), Quadrature (90°/270°) associated by Nelson with times of disrupted radio signals.
  • Harmonic aspects: Sextiles (60°) and Trines (120°), especially between massive planets like Jupiter & Saturn, claimed to be associated with more stable propagation.
  • Empirical claims: Nelson claimed ~80-90% accuracy in data sets from 1950-51 for certain conditions. Critics point out lack of modern statistical controls, unknown data biases, and no established physical mechanism widely accepted.
  • Relevance: Even if not scientifically proven, it shares with astrology the idea that planetary geometry and timing matter; useful for illustrating how pattern recognition shows up in different domains.
