John H. Nelson’s Planetary Propagation Theory

John H. Nelson’s work is a fascinating blend of astronomy, radio science, and planetary theory. He explored how planetary alignments might influence solar activity and, in turn, radio wave propagation on Earth.

Nelson’s Key Findings

  • Developed a theory in the 1950s that planetary positions relative to the Sun could predict shortwave radio propagation conditions.
  • Observed that heliocentric angles of 0°, 90°, 180°, and 270° correlated with degraded radio propagation.
  • Angles like 60° and 120°, especially between Jupiter and Saturn, were linked to stable propagation.
  • Predictions were reportedly ~85% accurate based on data from 1950–1951.

Why RCA Was Interested

RCA relied heavily on shortwave radio for global communications. Magnetic storms, often triggered by solar activity, could disrupt these signals. Nelson’s goal was to create a forecasting system using planetary configurations as indicators.

Legacy and Controversy

Republished in Infinite Energy magazine in 2000, his work sparked renewed interest among amateur radio enthusiasts and alternative science communities. Though speculative, some researchers still explore it.

Astrology: The Cosmic Language

Astrology interprets planetary movements as symbolic messages, connecting the macrocosm (universe) with the microcosm (human life). It offers a framework for understanding personality, life patterns, and cosmic timing.

  • Planets: Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Rahu/Ketu(Nodes).
  • Houses: Twelve divisions of the sky representing life areas such as career, relationships, and health.
  • Zodiac Signs: Aries through Pisces, marking the Sun’s yearly path.

Linking Nelson's Degrees to Astrology

Nelson focused on planetary angles of 0°, 60°, 90°, 120°, 180°, and 270°. In astrology, these are key aspects:

  • 0° (Conjunction): Planets merge energies—can intensify influence.
  • 60° (Sextile): Harmonious opportunity and easy flow.
  • 90° (Square): Tension, challenge, dynamic growth.
  • 120° (Trine): Natural talent, effortless support.
  • 180° (Opposition): Balance or conflict between polarities.
  • 270°: A waning square, signaling internal adjustment.

These angular relationships that Nelson linked to radio disturbances mirror astrological interpretations of planetary aspects, highlighting a fascinating bridge between empirical observation and the cosmic language.
