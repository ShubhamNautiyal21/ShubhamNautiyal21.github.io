John H. Nelson’s Planetary Propagation Theory

John H. Nelson’s work is a fascinating blend of astronomy, radio science, and planetary theory. He wasn’t researching planets in the traditional astrophysical sense—like studying their geology or atmospheres—but rather exploring how planetary alignments might influence solar activity and, in turn, radio wave propagation on Earth.

Nelson’s Key Findings

  • In the early 1950s, while working as an engineer for RCA Communications, Nelson developed a theory that planetary positions relative to the Sun could be used to predict shortwave radio propagation conditions.
  • He observed that certain heliocentric angles between planets—especially 0°, 90°, 180°, and 270°—correlated with degraded radio propagation.
  • Angles like 60° and 120°, particularly between Jupiter and Saturn, were associated with stable propagation.
  • His predictions were reportedly about 85% accurate based on data from 1950–1951.

Why RCA Was Interested

RCA relied heavily on shortwave radio for global communications. Magnetic storms—often triggered by solar activity—could disrupt these signals. Nelson’s goal was to create a forecasting system for magnetic storms using planetary configurations as indicators.

Legacy and Controversy

His work was republished in Infinite Energy magazine in 2000, sparking renewed interest among amateur radio enthusiasts and alternative science communities. Despite its intriguing correlations, Nelson’s theory hasn’t gained mainstream scientific acceptance. It’s often viewed as speculative, though some researchers still find it worth exploring.

Astrology: The Cosmic Language

Astrology interprets planetary movements as symbolic messages connecting the macrocosm (universe) with the microcosm (human life). It offers a framework for understanding personality, life patterns, and cosmic timing.

  • Planets: Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, plus outer planets for modern charts.
  • Houses: Twelve divisions of the sky representing life areas such as career, relationships, and health.
  • Zodiac Signs: Aries through Pisces, marking the Sun’s yearly path.

Linking Nelson's Degrees to Astrology

Nelson focused on planetary angles of 0°, 60°, 90°, 120°, 180°, and 270°. In astrology, these are key aspects:

  • 0° (Conjunction): Planets merge energies—can intensify influence.
  • 60° (Sextile): Harmonious opportunity and easy flow.
  • 90° (Square): Tension, challenge, dynamic growth.
  • 120° (Trine): Natural talent, effortless support.
  • 180° (Opposition): Balance or conflict between polarities.
  • 270°: A waning square, signaling internal adjustment.

These same angular relationships that Nelson linked to radio disturbances mirror astrological interpretations of planetary aspects, highlighting an intriguing bridge between empirical observation and ancient cosmic language.
